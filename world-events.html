While You Were in Stillwater

The World
1982–1988

The events you remember. The moments that defined a decade.

1982

Doug's Freshman Year

You arrived at Oklahoma State. E.T. phoned home. The world was in the Cold War. Here's what was happening.

E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial

Steven Spielberg's film surpassed Star Wars to become the highest-grossing film of all time. Four Academy Awards.

Falklands War

Argentina invaded the Falkland Islands. Britain sent a naval task force. The 74-day conflict ended with a British victory and over 900 dead.

Vietnam Veterans Memorial

November 13: Maya Lin's V-shaped black-granite wall with 57,939 names was dedicated in Washington, D.C. A nation finally honoring its veterans.

One Million March in NYC

June 12: One million people marched in New York for an end to the Cold War arms race — the largest protest in American history at that time.

Tylenol Murders

Seven people in Chicago died from cyanide-laced Tylenol capsules. The case remains unsolved but forever changed product packaging in America.

AT&T Breakup

The agreement to break up "Ma Bell" was finalized, splitting the telephone monopoly into regional "Baby Bells."

1983

Sophomore Year • Basketball Goes 24-7

The Cowboys made the NCAA Tournament for the first time in 18 years. The world almost ended. And Michael Jackson moonwalked.

M*A*S*H Finale

February 28: "Goodbye, Farewell and Amen" drew 121.6 million viewers — still one of the most-watched TV episodes in history.

Michael Jackson's Moonwalk

March 25: Jackson debuted the moonwalk during "Billie Jean" on Motown 25. It became the most recognizable dance move on the planet.

Beirut Barracks Bombing

October 23: Terrorist attacks killed 241 U.S. Marines and 58 French troops in Lebanon — one of the deadliest days for the Marines since Iwo Jima.

Korean Air Lines Flight 007

September 1: Soviet fighters shot down a commercial 747 that strayed into Soviet airspace. All 269 aboard were killed, including a U.S. Congressman.

Sally Ride

June 18: Became the first American woman in space aboard Challenger's STS-7 mission.

CDs Launch in America

March 2: The compact disc officially launched in the U.S. Also this year: Microsoft Word released. The first mobile phone hit the market.

1984

Junior Year • Football Goes 10-2

Reagan won 49 states. Mary Lou Retton scored a perfect 10. The Mac changed computing. The Cowboys had their best season ever.

Reagan's Landslide

November 6: Reagan defeated Walter Mondale in one of history's biggest landslides — 49 states, 525 electoral votes. Geraldine Ferraro became the first woman on a major party ticket.

LA Olympics

Mary Lou Retton scored a perfect 10 on her final vault, becoming the first American woman to win Olympic gymnastics gold. The Soviets boycotted.

Apple Macintosh

January 24: The Mac launched with the iconic "1984" Super Bowl commercial directed by Ridley Scott. Seen by 96 million viewers.

Indira Gandhi Assassinated

October 31: India's Prime Minister was shot by two of her own bodyguards, triggering a national crisis.

Bhopal Disaster

December 3: Chemical leak at a Union Carbide plant in India killed an estimated 15,000–20,000 people — the worst industrial disaster in history.

Oklahoma Drinking Age Raised

The legal drinking age went from 18 to 21. Eskimo Joe's added a restaurant. The Strip would never be the same.

1985

Senior Year • Undergrad Graduation

Doug earned his business degree. The world tried to save Africa. New Coke was a disaster. And they found the Titanic.

Live Aid

July 13: Simultaneous concerts at Wembley (London) and JFK Stadium (Philadelphia) raised over $100 million for Ethiopian famine relief. Watched by 1.5 billion people. Queen stole the show.

Farm Aid

September 22: Willie Nelson, Neil Young, and John Mellencamp headlined the first Farm Aid in Illinois. 80,000 people. Over $9 million raised for American farmers.

"We Are the World"

January 28: USA for Africa's charity single became a cultural phenomenon. Michael Jackson and Lionel Richie wrote it. 46 artists recorded it in one night.

Reagan Meets Gorbachev

November 19–21: The Geneva Summit — the first meeting between the two leaders. A meaningful step toward easing Cold War tensions.

New Coke Disaster

April 23: Coca-Cola changed its formula. America revolted. The original returned in less than 3 months as "Coca-Cola Classic."

Titanic Discovered

September 1: A joint American-French expedition found the wreck of the RMS Titanic on the ocean floor, 73 years after it sank.

1986

Doug Starts Grad School

January 28, 1986 — The Challenger Disaster

Doug, you mentioned this one. You'd just started graduate school. At 11:39 AM Eastern, 73 seconds after liftoff, the Space Shuttle Challenger broke apart 46,000 feet above the Atlantic. All seven crew members were killed, including schoolteacher Christa McAuliffe. Millions of schoolchildren were watching live.

The cause: two rubber O-rings in the right solid rocket booster failed in the record-setting cold (temperatures in the 20s°F at launch). NASA wouldn't send astronauts into space for more than two years.

"I remember where I was." — Everyone who lived through it.

Chernobyl

April 26: Reactor 4 at Chernobyl, Ukraine exploded, releasing radioactive material across Europe. The worst nuclear disaster in history. 350,000+ forcibly resettled.

Iran-Contra Breaks

November: A Lebanese magazine revealed the U.S. had been secretly selling weapons to Iran. Oliver North began shredding documents. Reagan addressed the nation.

Top Gun

May 16: Tom Cruise as "Maverick" became a superstar. The highest-grossing film of 1986. Navy recruitment spiked 500%.

Oprah Goes National

September 8: The Oprah Winfrey Show was broadcast nationally for the first time, launching one of the most influential media careers in history.

Hands Across America

May 25: ~6 million Americans held hands in a coast-to-coast human chain to raise money for homelessness.

Halley's Comet Returns

April 11: Halley's Comet made its closest approach to Earth (39 million miles), returning after 76 years. The last time it would be visible until 2061.

1987

Grad School • Football Goes 10-2

The stock market crashed. A baby fell in a well. And the whole country watched Iran-Contra hearings on TV.

Black Monday

October 19: The Dow dropped 508 points — 22.6% in one day. The largest single-day decline in history. All 23 major world markets fell. Worldwide losses: $1.71 trillion.

Baby Jessica

October 14–16: 18-month-old Jessica McClure fell 22 feet down an 8-inch well in Midland, Texas. After 58 hours, the nation watched her rescue live. Reagan said "everybody became her godparents."

Iran-Contra Hearings

May–August: Televised Congressional hearings gripped the nation. Oliver North testified. The scandal exposed secret weapons deals and activities prohibited by Congress.

Wall Street (The Movie)

Oliver Stone's film gave us Gordon Gekko and "Greed is good." Michael Douglas won the Oscar. It defined the era's relationship with money and power.

Gallagher-Iba Renamed

The OSU arena was renovated, air-conditioned, and officially renamed Gallagher-Iba Arena — honoring Hank Iba while Doug was on campus.

PTL Scandal

Televangelist Jim Bakker's ministry fell apart amid financial fraud and a scandal involving Jessica Hahn. It captivated the media for months.

1988

Doug Graduates • Sanders Wins Heisman

Doug earned his master's degree. Barry Sanders ran wild. George Bush became president. And the decade began winding down.

Barry Sanders Wins the Heisman

Doug's graduation year. Barry Sanders set 34 NCAA records, rushed for 2,628 yards and 37 touchdowns, and won the Heisman Trophy in a landslide. He accepted via satellite from Tokyo. Read the full story →

George H.W. Bush Elected

November 8: Vice President Bush defeated Michael Dukakis, winning 40 states and 426 electoral votes. The Reagan era officially ended.

Pan Am Flight 103 (Lockerbie)

December 21: A Boeing 747 exploded over Lockerbie, Scotland. 270 killed — 259 on the plane and 11 on the ground. Libya was suspected.

Seoul Olympics & Ben Johnson

September: Canadian sprinter Ben Johnson won the 100m in 9.79 seconds, then tested positive for steroids. Stripped of gold. "The Dirtiest Race in History."

Kirk Gibson's Home Run

October 15: Hobbled by injuries, Gibson hit a pinch-hit walk-off homer off Dennis Eckersley in Game 1 of the World Series. One of the greatest moments in baseball history.

Yellowstone Burns

The largest wildfire in Yellowstone's history burned 793,880 acres (36% of the park). 25,000+ firefighters. Didn't end until the first September snows.

Soviets Leave Afghanistan

May 15: After 8+ years of war, the Soviet Army began its withdrawal. A major Cold War turning point. The full withdrawal was completed in February 1989.

The Culture of the '80s

The events shaped the decade. But the music, movies, fashion, and tech — that's what it felt like to live through it.
